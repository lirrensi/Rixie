You are writing the abstract — the thumbnail summary that sits on top. Your job is to compress everything below into one dense, sharp summary.

Think of this as the answer to: "What is this book actually about, and why does it matter?" In 100 words or fewer.

---

## What to capture

The core idea — the one thing this book is about, the seed everything else grows from.

The main perceptual shift — how this book changes how the reader sees the world, themselves, or a problem.

The primary payoff — what the reader can actually do or understand after reading this that they couldn't before.

---

## How to write it

Write in flowing prose. No bullet points, no structure.

Be ruthlessly selective. This is not a summary of the whole book — it's the essence, compressed.

Prioritize meaning over coverage. If a concept is peripheral, cut it. If it's load-bearing, keep it visible.

Use clear, punchy language. Every sentence should do work.

---

## Format

Write in the same language as the source. Just flowing prose from start to finish.

Length: 100 words maximum. Make each one count.
